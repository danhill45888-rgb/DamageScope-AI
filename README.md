# DamageScope-AI
AI-Assisted Forensic Building Inspection and Documentation Platform
What is DamageScope AI?

DamageScope AI is an AI-assisted forensic building inspection and documentation platform that helps property professionals capture, organize, analyze, and document property conditions through a structured, evidence-based workflow. Developed from decades of real-world insurance adjusting and property-loss experience, DamageScope AI combines mobile field inspections with desktop forensic analysis to create a complete chain of evidence—from the initial inspection to the final professional report.

Rather than functioning as a simple image classifier or chatbot, DamageScope AI is designed to understand buildings, building components, and the relationships between photographs, locations, observations, measurements, findings, and supporting evidence. By combining expert knowledge with OpenAI technologies, the platform helps professionals perform more consistent inspections, generate defensible documentation, and preserve the reasoning behind every conclusion.

What Problem Does DamageScope AI Solve?

Property inspections often involve hundreds of photographs, notes, measurements, and observations collected under demanding field conditions. Important evidence can be missed, photographs may become disorganized, findings may vary from one inspector to another, and preparing a comprehensive report can require hours of manual work.

DamageScope AI addresses these challenges by guiding inspectors through a structured inspection process, automatically organizing evidence, assisting with building-component identification, supporting evidence-based findings, and generating professional reports that clearly connect each conclusion to its supporting documentation.

A key differentiator is DamageScope AI's evidence-first forensic reasoning approach. Instead of simply asking, "What is shown in this image?", the platform evaluates observable evidence, considers multiple possible explanations, rejects unsupported conclusions, and maintains traceability between every finding and the evidence that supports it. Expert corrections are preserved to improve future performance through a continuous learning architecture while keeping qualified professionals in control of the final decisions.
